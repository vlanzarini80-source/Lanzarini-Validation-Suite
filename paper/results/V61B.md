# V61B — Dense Frontier Interpretation

## Scope

This document summarizes the preserved conclusions obtained from the V61B dense frontier analysis.

Only experimentally supported observations are reported.

No additional measurements or reconstructed results are introduced.

---

## Purpose

The objective of V61B was to interpret the experimentally observed backend-selection frontier obtained from the previous benchmark campaign.

The analysis was based on measured runtime results collected on the evaluated experimental grid.

---

## Experimental Context

Hardware:

- GPU: NVIDIA A100-SXM4-80GB
- Precision: FP16

Compared implementations:

- Lanzarini Sparse-Local Kernel
- FlashAttention Sliding-Window

Measurement type:

- Forward runtime only

---

## Preserved Experimental Conclusions

The preserved analysis indicates that:

- no single threshold correctly separates all measured configurations;
- the transition between Lanzarini and FlashAttention is not represented by a simple one-dimensional rule;
- the observed behavior is better described as a two-dimensional empirical frontier.

---

## Interpretation

The measured benchmark campaign indicates that backend selection depends on the evaluated experimental configuration.

The observed frontier is empirical.

It is derived only from experimentally evaluated points.

It should not be interpreted as an analytical law.

---

## Scientific Limitations

V61B does not establish:

- a universal routing law;
- hardware-independent behavior;
- correctness outside the measured configurations;
- applicability to different GPUs;
- applicability to different precisions;
- applicability to different sequence lengths that were not experimentally evaluated.
