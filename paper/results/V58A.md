# V58A — FlashAttention vs Lanzarini Head-to-Head Benchmark

## Purpose

This checkpoint reports the preserved experimental results of the first systematic runtime comparison between the Lanzarini sparse-local attention kernel and FlashAttention Sliding-Window.

The objective was to compare both implementations under identical experimental conditions.

---

## Hardware

- GPU: NVIDIA A100-SXM4-80GB
- Precision: FP16

---

## Compared Backends

- Lanzarini Sparse-Local Kernel
- FlashAttention Sliding-Window

---

## Experimental Configuration

The benchmark was executed using identical experimental conditions for both implementations.

Only experimentally measured runtimes were considered.

No analytical estimates or reconstructed values are reported.

---

## Preserved Results

The benchmark identified configurations where:

- Lanzarini achieved lower runtime;
- FlashAttention achieved lower runtime.

These experimentally observed results motivated the subsequent investigation of an empirical backend-selection frontier.

---

## Interpretation

V58A demonstrated that neither backend universally dominates the other.

Instead, performance depends on the evaluated experimental configuration.

This observation motivated the later frontier studies documented in V58B, V58E, V59A and V60A-LITE.

---

## Limitations

This checkpoint does not establish:

- universal performance superiority;
- a hardware-independent rule;
- performance outside the tested configurations;
- applicability to different GPUs or precisions.
